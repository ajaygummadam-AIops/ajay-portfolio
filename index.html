Scrum Master Playbook

  • Retro Formats: Start/Stop/Continue · 4Ls · Mad-Sad-Glad · Sailboat

Ideas & Insights

My Thoughts on Agile & Product

Perspectives shaped by real-world delivery of IT and SaaS — across sprint teams, client rollouts, and shifting stakeholder expectations.

Scrum · SaaS
Why Scrum Works Better in Product Companies Than in Services
In services, every client is a product. I've run sprints where two clients had conflicting release windows in the same week. The Two-Track Sprint Model — separating internal product work from client delivery — was the only thing that kept both streams healthy without burning the team.
Ajay Kumar Agile · SaaS
Delivery · Hybrid
How I Managed Three Active SaaS Rollouts Without Dropping a Single Sprint
At Edusys, I was running parallel delivery for three client accounts simultaneously. What kept it together wasn't heroics — it was a shared RAID log updated every sprint, a weekly cross-client dependency check, and stakeholders who trusted the process because I never let them be surprised.
Ajay Kumar Delivery · Governance
Power BI · Reporting
The Dashboard That Stopped Every "What's the Status?" Meeting
When I connected Azure DevOps to Power BI at Syvora, the weekly status call dropped from 45 minutes to 15. Leadership could see sprint health, SLA trends, and risk RAG in real time. Reporting is not a deliverable — it is the foundation of stakeholder trust.
Ajay Kumar Power BI · Analytics
Retrospectives · Continuous Improvement
The Retro That Changed How We Onboard Every New Client
Three clients in a row had the same issue — a configuration step that nobody owned clearly, causing a three-week delay each time. One honest retrospective surfaced it. We built a client onboarding checklist from that session, and we never hit that delay again. That is what retrospectives are for.
Ajay Kumar Process Improvement
Agile Leadership · Culture
Scrum Values Are Not Ceremonies — They Are a Culture
Commitment, Courage, Focus, Openness, Respect. I have seen teams run all five ceremonies perfectly and still fail to deliver. The difference is always whether the values are lived in the daily standup, not just recited in the team charter. Ceremonies are the container. Values are what you pour in.
Ajay Kumar Leadership · Culture
Impediments · Servant Leadership
Impediment Removal Is 80% Listening and 20% Fixing
Most blockers that land in a standup are symptoms, not causes. I learned early that jumping to a fix before understanding the real issue wastes two sprints instead of one. The best question I ever asked was simply: "When did this first feel like a problem?" The answer always pointed somewhere upstream.
Ajay Kumar Scrum Master
